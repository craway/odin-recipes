Spaghetti Bolognese

Description

Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian-inspired dish that consists of spaghetti pasta served with a rich and flavorful meat-based sauce known as Bolognese. Originating from the region of Bologna in Italy, the sauce is typically made with ground beef (or a mixture of beef and pork), tomatoes, onions, carrots, celery, and herbs, all simmered slowly to create a savory, hearty sauce. This comforting and filling dish is a favorite around the world, often topped with grated Parmesan cheese for extra richness.

Ingredients

  • Pasta
  • Minced Meat
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Basel
  • Water
  • Tomato Paste
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Boil the water
    • Fill a pot with water, add a pinch of salt, and bring it to a boil.
    • Once boiling, add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions (usually around 8-10 minutes).
    • Drain the pasta once cooked and set it aside.
  2. Cook the minced meat
    • In a large pan or skillet, heat a bit of oil.
    • Add the minced meat and cook on medium heat, breaking it up as it browns.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook the meat until it's fully browned and no longer pink.
  3. Add tomato paste and seasoning
    • Once the meat is fully browned, lower the heat and stir in 1-2 tablespoons of tomato paste.
    • Cook the tomato paste with the meat for about 2-3 minutes, allowing it to blend and cook down slightly.
    • Add a bit of water to loosen the sauce, adjusting the consistency to your liking.
    • Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and a pinch of basil to enhance the flavors.
    • Let the sauce simmer for about 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  4. Combine pasta and sauce
    • Once the sauce is ready, you can add the drained pasta to the pan with the sauce.
    • Toss everything together until the pasta is well-coated with the flavors from the meat and basil.
